Chapter 02 · The pressure resolves

See what
takes space.

PhoneSpace separates a crowded library into categories that can be understood and reviewed deliberately.

01 · CategoryPhotosSimilar, duplicates, large, old
02 · CategoryVideosAll videos and the largest files
03 · CategoryContactsDuplicates and incomplete entries
04 · CategoryFilesFolders and downloads to review
01 · Pressure

A full library first feels like one problem.

The opening state keeps categories close together: present, useful, but difficult to read as a system.

02 · Awareness

The ring gives every category a boundary.

Photos, videos, contacts, and files separate into a clear overview without leaving the device.

03 · Review

Nothing happens before you choose.

PhoneSpace surfaces material for review. It does not turn organization into an invisible destructive action.

04 · Room

The interface emerges. The pressure recedes.

A crowded device becomes an understandable library with clear places to look next.

Chapter 03 · Review before acting

Decisions stay
with you.

The product organizes what is worth reviewing. Selection and cleanup remain deliberate.

01Photos

Find the visual noise.

Review screenshots, large photos, similar photos, duplicate groups, Live Photos, low-resolution media, and older items in separate lists.

02Videos

Start with what is large.

See all videos and the largest files together, with their size available before you decide what to do.

03Contacts + files

Bring order beyond media.

Review duplicate or incomplete contacts, repeated numbers, and folders in your downloads. Contact cleanup includes backup and restore.

Control point

Review first. Confirm next. Cleanup is never presented as automatic.

Photo deletion follows Apple’s system confirmation. Processing remains on the iPhone.
